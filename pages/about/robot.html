The Robot

Our robot was designed for the FIRST Robotics Competition (FRC). This page lists its components, functions, and design methodologies.

See the bot

Making The Bot

The season kicked off at the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor on January 7th. There we learned the rules of this year's competition, and did lots of other fun engineer-y things. At our first meetings afterward, we separated into teams and started planning our build. Some chose to work on construction, some on design, and others became specialists in the rules. Once we knew what our jobs were, we began to design and build the important additions to the existing chassis.

Initially drawing inspiration from the University of Michigan's Robot in 3 Days, we decided to use an arm with a claw on the end to grab and move the game pieces. After some research, we decided on a three-part mechanism: an elevator, an arm, and a claw. We began construction, and while there were a few hiccups along the way, in the end we had a chain-powered elevator lifting and lowering a belt-powered linear extender with a servo-powered claw on the end.

While programming the new parts was difficult, requiring more libraries and more complex code on our already-overworked tiny CPU, we were able to power the arm properly and control it smoothly. In our first competition, however, the arm was damaged, and we were forced to take on a more defensive role. We powered through, though, and placed highly for a school of our size.

Specifications

Components
  • Tank-drive 4-wheel chassis
  • 4-corner bumper, in blue & red
  • Elevator system to move arm
  • Linear slide acting as an arm
  • Robotic claw at the end of the arm
Functions & Features
  • Moves & brakes fast (top speed 20mph+)
  • Picks up game pieces (cones & cubes)
  • Places game pieces in scoring zones
  • Defends strongly, it's real big and heavy
  • Adheres to all FRC regulations
